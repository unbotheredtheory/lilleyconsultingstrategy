You Don’t Have to Be Ready. You Just Have to Be Informed.

5 Things You Can Say in Any Appointment:

  • “Can you explain what this form is for?” – Don’t sign anything without understanding it.
  • “I’d like to receive information in writing, please.” – You have the right to process things at your own pace.
  • “I’d like to add a note to my file that reflects my view.” – If something doesn’t sit right, it’s okay to speak up.
  • “Can I see my own records before I give consent to share them?” – Know what they know about you.
  • “I’m not comfortable answering that without knowing why it’s being asked.” – You can slow the process down.

Red Flags to Watch For:

  • “It’s just a standard form.” – But it might carry weight you weren’t told about.
  • “It’s easier if you just sign it now.” – Ask: easier for who?
  • “We can’t give you that.” – Ask for the policy in writing.

Your Personal Boundaries:

  • I prefer written / verbal / visual information.
  • Please don’t share my information without checking with me first.
  • I want to know about all test results, even if they’re “within range.”
  • I need time before making decisions.

Your Reminder:

You don’t have to say yes to be cooperative.
You don’t have to be grateful to be deserving.
You don’t have to perform trauma to be taken seriously.
